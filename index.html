The operating frame

Eduba works with agencies and professional services firms on exactly that question.

The operating frame is 60/30/10.

  • 60%
    Traditional code and database work.

    The unglamorous infrastructure that already has a right answer. Do not put a language model in front of it.

  • 30%
    Rule-based logic.

    Compliance gates, platform-policy branches, the kind of structured decision tree that political ad verification demands.

  • 10%
    Genuine AI problems.

    The places where a language model or a segmentation engine earns its keep.

Agencies lose money when they put AI where a checklist would do better and when they leave AI out of the places where variant production genuinely scales.

Applied to GAROI's stack specifically

Trilingual creative production

Mostly rule-based logic with small AI leverage on first-draft variants. The bottleneck is reviewer time, not copy generation.

Voter segmentation

A real AI problem. The methodology behind Race to 270 is productizable. Right now it sits in senior heads and gets re-derived each cycle.

Platform compliance

Meta authorization, Google verification, X certification, the separate rule set per cycle. A rules-plus-context problem where Interpretable Context Methodology pays off.

ICM is Jake's published work. Folder-as-architecture, measurable interpretability, used by a 52-member practitioner community and published in ACM TiiS. Read ICM on GitHub.
